Common Mental Health Concerns

Feeling Anxious

It's normal to feel nervous sometimes. Anxiety can cause worries, a fast heartbeat, or trouble sleeping.
Try: Slow, deep breaths or talking to someone you trust.

üòî

Feeling Sad

Everyone feels sad sometimes. If sadness lasts a long time, it might help to talk about your feelings.
Try: Doing something you enjoy or reaching out to a friend.

üò´

Feeling Stressed

Stress can make your body feel tense or your mind feel busy. Taking breaks and breathing can help.
Try: Stretching, drawing, or a quick walk outside.

If you ever feel overwhelmed, remember you are not alone. Talk to a trusted adult or visit our resources below.

Simple Mindful Exercise

Breathe In as you follow the first colour.
Breathe Out as you reach the other side.
Repeat for each colour!

5-4-3-2-1 Mindfulness

  • 5 things you can see
  • 4 things you can touch
  • 3 things you can hear
  • 2 things you can smell
  • 1 thing you can taste

What is Mental Health?

üòä

For Children

Mental health is how you think, feel, and act. Being mindful helps you notice your feelings and find calm when you are upset. Try: 5-4-3-2-1 breathing!

For Parents

Mindfulness builds resilience in children.
Encourage open conversations, practice breathing together, and model how to pause and notice feelings. Listening is powerful!

üè´

For Educators

Mindfulness in school helps children feel safe and ready to learn. Use short breathing or stretch breaks, and create a calm, positive classroom routine. You set the tone!
